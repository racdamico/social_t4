🏡 What are villages like?

People live in settlements with houses, streets and other buildings. The number of people is the population. Villages are small settlements with a small population. In the centre there is a main square with a town hall and a church. Streets are short and narrow. Buildings are low.

🏙️ What are towns and cities like?

Towns and cities are large settlements with a large population. Most cities have an old part called the historic centre (narrow streets, low buildings, monuments), a modern district (wide streets, tall buildings, offices) and suburbs (residential areas away from the centre with houses and factories).

🏛️ What is the local council?

Every village, town or city is a municipality. It has a local council that governs it. The workers are called councillors. The head of the council is the mayor. They work in the town hall. Citizens can vote every 4 years in elections.

🚌 What are local services?

The local council organises local services — services that citizens use in their municipality. There are four main types: police & fire (keep people safe), transport (public buses, etc.), cultural & leisure (museums, sports centres) and waste management & sanitation (keep city clean, clean water).

💰 What are municipal taxes?

The local council collects taxes to pay for local services. Taxes are money that citizens have to pay to the government. Property tax: citizens who own houses pay this. The money is used to keep streets clean, maintain sewers, build parks. Road tax: citizens who own cars or vehicles pay this. The money builds and repairs roads, installs traffic signs.
